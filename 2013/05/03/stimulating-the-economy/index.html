Stimulating the economy

May 3, 2013 by davald

The unemployment numbers continue to depress. While the private sector has added new jobs, they are insufficient to overcome population growth and those who have simply stopped looking for work. Writing for the New York Times, Binyamin Applebaum tells us why we shouldn’t get too excited about the headlines on job additions. He cites the data reflected in the following chart:

 

See, not much to cheer. Applebaum writes:

The share of adults looking for work peaked at 6.4 percent of the population in 2010. It fell to 4.7 percent in April. But recall that over the same period, the share of adults with jobs did not change. What grew instead is the share of adults no longer counted as part of the labor force.

Increasingly, the down-and-out seek solace in their own demise.

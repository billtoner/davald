Its not about those jobs not requiring college. It’s the fact that “college” is no longer a factor employers of the future will be looking for in what makes a candidate qualified for the job. Why? Well for one, so many people are going to college, most post secondary institutions are treating education like a business and the quality of the education is declining to allow for PROFITABILITY. What results from that? A college Degree isn’t worth much anymore. I’ve personally met countless University Grads who don’t have a lick of common sense or empirical knowledge to go with all their fancy degrees.
And I know a lot of really skilled capable workers with innovative ideas and so much value, who get turned away from jobs because they don’t have that fancy degree, even though you don’t need that degree or the knowledge acquired from it to do the actual job. Obviously those employers still have an old mindset about hiring.
The world of work is changing, and employers who are aware of the times and hiring for the future, are going to be demanding more, and demanding differently.

Whether one looks at Europe or right here at home in the US, data show that austerity is not what the economic doctor ordered for our present condition.

It just so happens that one side always has the same prescription for every economic situation: a market solution of lower taxes – preferably for the highest tax brackets and on income accruing to the wealthiest, less government, and no regulation. Whereas many Keynesians and others who do not subscribe to the efficient markets hypothesis often base their policy recommendations on economic conditions – their recommendations are far more nuanced, the other side seems to force economic conditions to fit their well-known biases. Witness the much-publicized excel “errors” that keep arising from conservative circles, both in academia and in business. Of course, these “errors” always seem to validate their inherent biases…

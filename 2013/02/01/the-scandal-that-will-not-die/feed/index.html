The Church understands the decline in numbers for entry into the Priesthood will cripple the organization over time, so it has financial incentive to “work with” the offending Priests vs. do the right thing. My feeling is that the hierarchy is in serious trouble and it knows it. It’s a similar situation in the ranks of the Republican faithful. Appeal to a narrow group while upholding your principles and die, or else else adapt to changing times. Tough one.

Having said that, I think the Church hierarchy has no idea how unnecessary it is to its flock. Should they disappear completely tomorrow, followers of the Catholic faith would still gather, say Mass w/o a Priest, recite The Creed, distribute communion and celebrate the other sacraments and barely notice that an official from the Vatican hierarchy is missing.

You bring up an excellent point in all of this, about the dilemma Catholics have in reconciling their political positions with their religious ones. There’s an inherent conflict between caring about social justice and adhering to Catholic doctrine. Which is exactly why I find it fascinating to observe so many of the faithful practicing the faith, while believing in their hearts that the Church hierarchy is completely full of shit on social issues like birth control, equal rights for women and gays — (especially in the church itself), and having a safety net for those of us not born on 3rd base like Mr. Romney.

That is the challenge, isn’t it? Start reconciling political vs. religious beliefs and see what you get. It’s a fun exercise. You might even get your Sundays back.

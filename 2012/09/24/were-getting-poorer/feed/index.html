On a global scale the U.S. has one of the lowest Gini indexes and therefore one of the most even distributions of wealth among its citizens of any country in the world.

That is not to say the U.S. doesn’t have a growing wealth gap. We do. A pre-tax Gini around .43 like in the 1990s appears to be optimum.

The decrease in median income in 2011 and FRED disposable income is more a reflection of the severe hangover left over from the Great Recession than an increase in the Gini index.

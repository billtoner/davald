And I’m absolutely fascinated by the ideas presented here. There’s been a lot of buzz in the communities I’ve been involved in where sharing a strong community matters more than rich or poor. You see the same in the homeschool co-op my family is a part of. Religion, income/wealth, and other differences are largely ignored in favor of more “important” things, making sure our children have wonderful childhoods filled with opportunities for learning. The knitting group I attend is very much the same. It’s a social group where people meet for a common interest and money isn’t regarded as a factor (unless you’re talking about how much yarn you’ve managed to buy!) It’s amazing how different that community is from the other social groups I’ve known where wealth or “status” are important.

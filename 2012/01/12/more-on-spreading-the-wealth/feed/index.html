Comments on: More on spreading the wealth https://davald.wordpress.com/2012/01/12/more-on-spreading-the-wealth/ Prima facie observations about anything Mon, 05 Mar 2012 16:25:17 +0000 hourly 1 http://wordpress.com/ By: Mark France https://davald.wordpress.com/2012/01/12/more-on-spreading-the-wealth/#comment-304 Mon, 05 Mar 2012 16:25:17 +0000 http://snohomishobserver.com/?p=2070#comment-304 One reason we “can’t afford” these things is that we have squandered trillions on unnecessary foreign wars. Blame mainly Bush, but partly Obama, and always Congress. The implication in the post above is that we should “share the gains” through taxation to fund, presumably, government programs to help the “rest of us”. I certainly agree that the rich could afford to pay more taxes, since “they” have already done so in the past. The most that could accomplish is to lower the interest on the national debt a little, which would be a good thing, but not translate into much, if any, more prosperity. I agree with most of the Republicans who say we don’t have a taxation problem, we have a spending problem. What we don’t need is more taxation that is used for more government programs ad infinitum. What we need for government to do is create a much more positive environment for business to operate in, because flourishing businesses are what create good jobs and more prosperity for “the rest of us”. Government is certainly necessary and we need to fund it with taxes, but government is bloated and off point currently. It seems to be attempting to “mandate” prosperity through legislation, taxation policy and regulation. Instead, it has produced the opposite effect. Government does some good and necessary things, but government taxation and borrowing have a net effect of removing money from the economy. Government can certainly redistribute what wealth there is, but always reduces the overall wealth in the process. A better way is for government to get out of the way of wealth production, which creates more wealth for everyone to share. Thriving businesses hire more people and pay higher wages.

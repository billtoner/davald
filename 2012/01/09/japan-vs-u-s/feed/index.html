]]> By: libertyblog91 https://davald.wordpress.com/2012/01/09/japan-vs-u-s/#comment-54 Tue, 10 Jan 2012 00:25:03 +0000 http://snohomishobserver.com/?p=2035#comment-54 Interesting, you must appreciate the difference between the structure of Japanese and US debt, in the Clinton era (one of the reasone for the balanced budget) the US changed the structure of its debt form mostly 10yr and 30yr bonds to t-bills with a 2yr maturation. This decreased the interest bill but leaves the US very vulnerable to market fluctuations as it has to turn over a large amount of debt every 2 years. The other difference is who owns the debt, in Japan it is mostly owned by Japanese citizens, indeed Japan runs large trade and current account surpluses, the US on the other hand has run massive current account and trade deficits for the last 30 years and much of its debt is owned by foreigners, including Japan. The Japanese government does have a high government debt/GDP ratio but the headline figures disguises the truth, Japan is in a much stronger economic position than the US in many respects and if the US with its current debt structure owed as much as Japan does, it would not be sustainable. Interesting graphs but in this respect you offer a false comparison.

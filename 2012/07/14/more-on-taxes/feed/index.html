Comments on: More on taxes https://davald.wordpress.com/2012/07/14/more-on-taxes/ Prima facie observations about anything Sun, 06 Jan 2013 14:09:18 +0000 hourly 1 http://wordpress.com/ By: Dave https://davald.wordpress.com/2012/07/14/more-on-taxes/#comment-1955 Sun, 06 Jan 2013 14:09:18 +0000 http://snohomishobserver.com/?p=3767#comment-1955 I was out doing some tax research and stumbled on this blog post, and thought you’d be amused (given your dismissal in the body text of this possibility) that yes, someone in the top .01% earning above $7.9M did indeed read this — and even found it useful and engaging. Good work. Income disparity is an issue many of my wealthy friends actually do lament; shouldn’t we all want *everyone* to be wealthier, for a better community, especially directed at those at the very bottom? The debate should be over how we get there. My concern is that we’re looking only at ways to redistribute a fixed (or shrinking) pie, rather than expanding the whole pie — and in a way that benefits the lower 90% at a greater rate than the upper 10%. To me, the best way to achieve that is through trade and industrial policy, to repatriate solid middle-class jobs here from Asia — where admittedly the people in my income bracket gleefully sent those jobs because they were rewarded for short term profit by the stock markets, and by government (under both parties). Walk into any typical small town in America, and you can see that the middle-tier shops, homes, and so on date from the 60’s and 70’s generally, indicating the flatness of the inflation-adjusted income curve for the bottom 90%. It sickens me. Essentially, “free trade” wasn’t “free”, and sucked out jobs from the lower classes which we must get back onshore — or levy duties on foreign goods to compensate for that loss — if we want the whole pie to grow again, and to do so for the benefit of common working class people instead of just the wealthy.

By the 16th Amendment Congress has the power to impose taxes on income, regardless of source. Congress then buys things with the revenues collected. We have little, if any, choice as to what Congress buys, which includes a wide array of goods and services, from guns and ammo to health care for the aged and Social Security. One might say that we are not directly buying these items. We are buying them nonetheless. We could call these “mandated purchases,” in so far as we cannot lawfully refuse to pay the tax or decide what the government buys with the tax dollars collected.

Also, if it were indeed unconstitutional for the government to “force me to buy something,” how can the individual states “force me to buy” car insurance as a condition for driving a car? Moreover, how can the government, at any level, “force me to buy” public education, or ferries, or roads, or police, or a court system—if it is unconstitutional to “force me to buy something”?

We have a distinction without a legal difference, in my humble opinion. In the end, however, it really doesn’t matter what any of us thinks on this subject. The Supreme Court will have the last word, for better or worse.

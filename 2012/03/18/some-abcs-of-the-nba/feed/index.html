Comments on: Some ABCs of the NBA https://davald.wordpress.com/2012/03/18/some-abcs-of-the-nba/ Prima facie observations about anything Sun, 18 Mar 2012 21:42:16 +0000 hourly 1 http://wordpress.com/ By: Bill Toner https://davald.wordpress.com/2012/03/18/some-abcs-of-the-nba/#comment-368 Sun, 18 Mar 2012 21:42:16 +0000 http://snohomishobserver.com/?p=2827#comment-368 I think Nate was a magician in Portland. His team was in the infirmary half the time and he was pulling wins from a young roster that really wasn’t quite ready for prime-time. The mark of a great coach is one who gets the most out of this players and Nate did that in spades, and I’m sure he will again somewhere else under better circumstances.

I hate to see the front office, led by Larry Miller get a pass in these situations. I have seen too many times where the Sr. Execs screw things up because they tinker with the roster. Nate’s an old fashioned coach in terms of earning minutes, accountability, etc. Felton and Wallace weren’t with the program. So the Sr. Execs don’t give Nate the type of players he needs to be successful and then make him the fall-guy.

Not long ago the biggest question about the Blazers was one of being too deep at each position and where were the minutes going to come from? The front office managed to make all the wrong moves and get magic beans in return. I don’t care if you’re Phil Jackson, you can’t go deep into the playoffs with that roster.

Miller should be accountable. Ultimately Paul Allen himself needs to find a guy he trusts to run the front office and back off of day to day operations, or else go down in history as the NBA’s own version of George Steinbrenner.

With the strike, I think a lot of the Portland faithful have lost interest in Blazer-mania. Those days are behind us. If you want to go where the enthusiasm is in Portland, you go to Jeld-Wen field and watch the Timbers, or else drive down to Eugene and watch the Ducks.

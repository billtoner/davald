Loker https://davald.wordpress.com/2011/08/29/do-facts-matter/#comment-30 Mon, 29 Aug 2011 22:05:53 +0000 http://snohomishobserver.com/?p=996#comment-30 The problem is neither Bush nor Obama. Also, while it may be hard to swallow, it is neither the debt nor the deficit. While these are very significant issues they are a result of another more deeply rooted problem. They are the symptoms of a hyper-inflated currency.

This is not to engage in bashing either Clinton or Bush, or Obama related to who had a surplus and who had a deficit. It is more important to understand that since 1972 we have cured these deficits, and created the surplusses, by printing as much money as was necessary to keep up.

What we had to keep up with was ever increasing spending, justified or not, and the problematic and continually increasing Trade Deficit. Since 1972 the trade deficit has blossomed to over $11 trillion cumulative total. Along the same period we have continued to expand the role of government and governmental spending. All of these decisions I think were made for what most thought were justified reasons. While it is also clear there were great partisan gains on both sides, the fundamental problem began when we jettisoned the gold standard in 1972.

We needed to get off the gold standard. It was disproportionality penalizing the US economy. We did not have to get off a standard though. There were other historically expanded standards like precisions metals that provided a better specie backing for the currency. But, it was easier and for some like the federal reserve, better from a control point to get off the standard all together. So Nixon, convinced of the correctness of the idea agreed and signed the law.

In 1972 we had about $500 billion of currency in circulation. Today we have between $10 trillion and $16 trillion depending on whose numbers you believe. This is at least a 2o time’s increase in the base net worth of the US if you were to believe it. Further if you look at where the money went during the period, it was allocated disproportionately to housing and health care as well as other entitlements. Those costs went up on par with the amount of currency while the other items in the economy did not follow pace.

So, today, we are left with an overvalued economic base. This is what is leading to the deficit and other problems. The core problem of the economy is this underlying issue and how we used the ability to print money unchecked to continue to expand programs and spending.

It is not really a partisan issue it is an American issue. As long as we continue to try to find a partisan win in this debacle we will fall into oblivion. We need to unite and change the nature of our economy and our own expectation of what we should get and give to government. In this I don’t mean either conservative or liberal ideology. I mean a pragmatic approach to balance what is ne3eded to protect the helpless and rebuild our economic engine. We will never get congress or our professional political class to do this as their business model is the antitheses of what is needed.
